How Does MOPITT Work?

MOPITT measures infrared radiation absorption caused by carbon monoxide (CO) in the atmosphere. It uses two spectral bands — near-infrared and thermal infrared — to construct vertical profiles of CO concentration from the surface up to about 15 km.

This allows tracking of emission sources (fires, traffic, industry) and how CO is transported globally.

Applications

  • Air quality monitoring
  • Smoke transport tracking
  • Climate monitoring
  • Model validation
