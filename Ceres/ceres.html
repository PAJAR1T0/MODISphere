CERES

What does CERES measure?

CERES measures how much solar energy reaches Earth and how much thermal energy is emitted back to space. This helps determine whether our planet is gaining or losing heat.

It also studies how clouds, aerosols, and surfaces (ice, oceans, forests) impact this balance.

Observation capabilities

  • Shortwave radiation (reflected)
  • Longwave radiation (emitted)
  • Global albedo

Technical sheet

Resolution: Global
Planet-wide coverage ideal for climate studies.
Bands: Shortwave/Longwave
Measures reflected solar (SW) and emitted thermal (LW) radiation.
Orbit: Terra
Instrument aboard NASA’s Terra satellite (polar orbit).

First CERES flew in December 1999 with Terra.
