ASTER

ASTER is an instrument designed to capture high-resolution images of Earth’s surface across multiple wavelengths. Its precision supports studies of topography, volcano morphology, urban expansion, glacier retreat, and environmental degradation. ASTER data are key to monitoring crustal changes and understanding the geologic and climate processes shaping our planet.

CERES

CERES measures Earth’s energy budget: how much solar radiation reaches the planet and how much is reflected or emitted back to space. This is essential for climate studies, revealing how clouds, aerosols, and surface types influence global warming. CERES data help build more accurate models of atmospheric behavior and the planet’s energy balance.

MISR

MISR observes Earth from nine distinct angles, enabling a 3D-like view of clouds, aerosols, and surfaces. It can measure cloud height, particle size, and surface/sea reflectance—powerful for studying air quality, wildfire smoke, and aerosol–climate interactions.

MODIS

MODIS provides daily global coverage, capturing over 30 environmental data products: wildfires, vegetation cover, sea-surface temperature, clouds, and sea-ice movement. Its continuous global record supports disaster response, agriculture, and climate-change monitoring.

MOPITT

MOPITT retrieves atmospheric composition, focusing on carbon monoxide and other pollutants. Scientists can trace pollution sources (fires, transport, industry) and track their dispersion. Its information is critical to understand air-pollution dynamics and impacts on health and climate.
